50 excuses to skip class: A guide to can't-miss activities this fall quarter


Living and studying in one of the busiest cities in the world, UCLA students have entertainment at their fingertips. Concerts, movie screenings, restaurant openings — the opportunities are endless in Los Angeles. But what if you still can't figure out what to do?

The Daily Bruin's Arts & Entertainment department sifted through countless activities and events to give you a handpicked guide for fall 2012. There's ideas for everyone, whether you are a brand-new freshman or a soon-to-be graduate. Of course, the Daily Bruin included classic Bruin traditions like getting an ice cream sandwich at Diddy Riese, as well as more obscure activities such as skating in a swimming pool-cum-ice rink.

With 50 listings below, you have exactly enough to make sure your next 10 weeks stay busy. And whenever you cross off events on the list below, be sure to share your photos at the Daily Bruin Arts & Entertainment Facebook page. Now, you're ready for fall quarter.

September


Sample the brews at L.A. Beer Week

Sample the brews at L.A. Beer Week


Courtesy of Cambria Griffith

Dates: Sept. 20-30
Cost: Prices vary by event
Where: Locations vary by event

It's a celebration of all things beer, from beer education to beer cuisine. Bars and restaurants all across Los Angeles are participating, and at the end of the week, the city brews a mean party with the 4th annual Los Angeles Beer Week Festival, where attendees are invited to taste creations from more than 70 different breweries.

Dance at the Bruin Bash concert

Dance at the Bruin Bash concert


Daily Bruin file photo

Date: Sept. 23

Where: UCLA Drake Stadium

There's no better way to start the year than letting loose in Drake Stadium. Join the crowd of undergraduates as they flood the stands in anticipation of this year's performances from Chiddy Bang, Ra Ra Riot and Los Rakas.

25th anniversary of "The Princess Bride"

25th anniversary of "The Princess Bride"


Courtesy of Robin Wright Penn

Date: Sept. 24
Cost: $9 for students
Where: Egyptian Theatre
6712 Hollywood Blvd., Los Angeles, CA 90028

"Hello. My name is Inigo Montoya. You killed my father. Prepare to die." Can you believe that fans of this memorable speech and the film that it came from have enjoyed Westley's quest to save his love, Buttercup, for 25 years now? Celebrate this classic story of adventure and good fun with a 98-minute screening and following discussion with Cary Elwes (Westley), Carol Kane (Valerie) and Wallace Shawn (Vizzini).

Perform a skit at The Improv Space

Get silly and showoff your acting chops in the Wednesday night series "The Improv Sessions." Even if you've never acted a day in your life, that's what improv is for. Walk on stage and perform by yourself or join a group, but be prepared for a good time and some laughs.

Venture to Griffith Observatory at night

Venture to Griffith Observatory at night


Daily Bruin file photo

Date: Anytime
Cost: Free
Where: 4730 Crystal Springs Drive, Los Angeles, CA 90027

Situated atop Mount Hollywood, the Griffith Observatory has some of the best views of Los Angeles and the stars above. The Observatory itself is an Angeleno icon, appearing in several films including "The Terminator" and "Rebel Without a Cause."
